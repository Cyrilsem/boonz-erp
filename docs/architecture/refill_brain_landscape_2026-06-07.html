2 · The refill journey — where you edit, add, modify

The core idea to internalize: planning is decoupled from inventory. The brain sizes every refill from shelf gaps (WEIMI live, what is physically on the machine), never from warehouse counts. So a wrong warehouse number can never stop a refill from being planned. Unreliable stock only bites later, at stitch and at packing, where the system flags it instead of silently under-filling.

Where the numbers drift — guard these
receive without a matching reservation does not debit the warehouse → phantom inflation. Always go through the canonical writers, never a manual count. · A WEIMI re-snapshot can shift a batch's expiry between pack and receive (handled by a trigger that follows the pinned batch). · consumer_stock is transit-only; warehouse questions use warehouse_stock. · Shelf counts come from v_live_shelf_stock, not pod_inventory. · Warehouse status changes are manager-only (propose-then-confirm).

How this ties back to unreliable inventory

The shelf node trusts WEIMI live, not the stored pod_inventory, which is why planning stays reliable even when warehouse counts are not. The warehouse node is the one with the weakest data, which is why the missing RD-02 (raise PO when short) and RD-06 (pick the source per line) matter most: they are the tools for acting when the warehouse number turns out to be wrong at stitch or pack time.
